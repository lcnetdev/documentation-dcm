**a. Not in LC**
The LC Database contains many records for items not held by LC. The presence of these
records stems from a variety of factors and programs that have been in place over a considerable
period of time. Such records are readily identifiable either by the legend "NOT IN LC
COLLECTIONS" (PCRD display) or 1 in the first indicator position of an 050 field (NCRD
display).  Occasionally the decision is made to select an item represented by a "not in LC"
record. The process of incorporating the item into the collections by adjusting the record and the
shelflist accordingly is called "adapting." At present, these materials are handled by the Policy
and Standards Division (PSD). These items are identified by a Report of Searcher Not in LC
Adapt slip (form 64-2c).
**b. NCCP**
The LC Database contains records that have either been initially input by participants in the
National Coordinated Cataloging Program (NCCP) or upgraded from an original LC in-process
record. The cataloging is usually completed prior to the time the item in LC is incorporated into
the collections. The procedure for doing this is the "NCCP adapt" procedure, which is done by
the cataloging teams according to the procedures in DCM L3. These items are identified by a
Report of Searcher NCCP Adapt slip (form 64-2b).
