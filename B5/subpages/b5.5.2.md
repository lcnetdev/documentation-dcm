When an item reaches a cataloger, it will usually be under initial bibliographic control,
meaning that it is represented by an in-process record in the IBC file, it will have been
searched (except for CIPs), and it will contain a priority slip indicating the division to which
the item was forwarded ("cataloging assignment") and possibly one or more other slips
indicating a variety of conditions, some of which may have been discovered during initial
searching (e.g., indication of an edition or translation).
When a team accepts an item for cataloging and logs it into its STARS database, a
STARS Slip is usually printed and accompanies an item through a team's workstream; see
DCM B5.7 (Appendix 2) for the guidelines in recording various kinds of information on
the STARS Slip.
