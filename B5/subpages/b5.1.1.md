Cataloging at the Library of Congress has undergone many changes over the years, both
administratively in the way it has been organized and substantively in terms of the use of new
technology, new cataloging rules, new cataloging practices, new cataloging programs, etc.
Despite these many changes, the fundamental components of cataloging have remained
relatively intact in regard to the descriptive aspect (descriptive cataloging), the subject aspect
(subject cataloging), and what, for want of a better term, will be called the inventory aspect
(shelflisting).
Until 1969 the general cataloging environment was that of what is sometimes referred to as
the "manual" mode, meaning that, aside from typewriters and similar devices, all the aspects of
the bibliographic control system were carried out by the staff directly, with relatively little
assistance from technology.
