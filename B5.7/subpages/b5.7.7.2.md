The procedures vary, depending upon whether an item is received in a team to do the
subject/shelflisting aspects only (anticipated subject-only) or whether the subject cataloger
discovers, in the course of doing the subject aspect, that the item is out of the team's scope
(unanticipated descriptive-only).
**a. Anticipated subject-only**
At the time the subject aspect is done the item contains a STARS Slip for the team doing the
subject aspect. (If the STARS Slip from the team doing the descriptive aspect inadvertently
accompanies the item, return that slip to the forwarding team). It also contains a Materials
Routing slip (in the case of serials a Serials Work Sheet/Routing Notice) from the unit that did
the descriptive aspect which may contain information for the use of the subject cataloger such as
a collected set call number, information on series treatment, name headings provided for subject
cataloging use, etc. The person doing the subject aspect records/checks data on the STARS Slip
as follows:
**Statistics area:** Records in *arabic numerals* the number of subject authorities
made/changed; the number of class numbers established/changed; the number of bibliographic
records changed.
**Call number, etc.:** Records the call number for the shelflister (in the case of collected sets,
records the call number provided on the Materials Routing slip that accompanies the item
forwarded for the subject aspect only); in the blank area below the collected set call number,
records any alternate class number for an analytic in a collected set.
*Completion area:*

1. indicates the appropriate source category (see DCM B5.7.5.3.b above) for items
descriptively cataloged outside LC Washington, e.g., by an LC overseas office, by
NLM for certain CIP materials; this is done by the person doing the subject aspect
because it is the first opportunity to indicate source;
2. indicates, as necessary, the activity completed, if it is not identical to the intended
activity for which the item was logged in. This rarely happens in anticipated
subject-only cataloging, but occasionally an item cannot be shelflisted by the team doing
the subject cataloging.

**b. Unanticipated descriptive-only**
When the subject cataloger determines that the item cannot be completed in the team but
instead must be forwarded to another one, he/she does the following:
for example, if an item received for full cataloging is discovered to be out of the
team's subject scope, the subject cataloger checks "Descriptive only" as the activity
completed in the team;

2. fills out a Materials Routing slip to accompany the item being forwarded; provides, as
necessary, on that slip collected set and other information for the person who will be
doing the subject aspect;
3. forwards the item, and routes the STARS Slip for log-out according to team practice.
