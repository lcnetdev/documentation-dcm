Occasionally when an item is still in the acquisition stage (recognizable by the fact that fixed field
1 in the APIF record contains value "a"), it is not selected upon receipt and is instead returned to the
vendor for credit (applies mainly to items that are purchased).  When this occurs, the decision is noted in
the bibliographic record as follows:

1. since the item is not to be added to the collections, the 050 field containing the in-process
legend "ACQUISITION IN PROCESS" is deleted from the acquisition record; this
generates the display constant "NOT IN LC COLLECTION" in brief record displays and
in the header of full record displays;
2. a 920 field (Selection decision) is added containing the "Do not acquire" legend, the
selecting official's initials, and the date of the decision.
Example:  920   $a Do not acquire per xyz 08-16-91
These steps prevent reordering of unwanted material and also inform users immediately that the item is
not intended for the Library's collections.  The placement of the 920 field at the head of the record in
MUMS records (PCRD/NCRD displays; APIF/BOOKSM input/update systems) makes the information
readily available and minimizes its being overlooked.
