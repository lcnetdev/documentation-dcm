The MLC shelf number has three possible components, each separated by a space:

1. the letters “MLC”, a letter representing size, and if applicable, a letter
representing a custodial assignment, e.g., MLCS or MLCMA;
2. a number consisting of the year of cataloging, a slash, and a sequential five-digit
number within each size, e.g., 2005/00456;
3. a one-letter LC classification assignment (enclosed within parentheses).
*Note:* Before the year 2000, a two-digit year number was used (e.g., MLCS 99/0234).
Beginning in 2000, the year number contained four digits (e.g., MLCM 2000/00789).
