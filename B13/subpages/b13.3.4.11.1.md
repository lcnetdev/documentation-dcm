The term "LCC-based number" is used to indicate a number based on Library of Congress
Classification/ *Classification Web* but assigned externally by the original cataloging library.  It is
a means of distinguishing such numbers from the final LC classification/call numbers chosen
(either the one externally assigned and accepted or one assigned by LC).
Each final record issued by LC must have an LC call number.  There are several ways to
obtain it, depending upon section practice and the cataloging stream being followed.  See
B13.3.4.11.2-4 below for details.  If a call number cannot be supplied by one of the methods
described, refer, as needed, the resource to a person within the section/division with subject
cataloging expertise.
