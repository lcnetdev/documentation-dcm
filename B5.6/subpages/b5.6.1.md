The Acquisitions and Bibliographic Access Directorate (ABA) was reorganized in 2008 to
integrate acquisitions and cataloging functions into units called sections.  The reorganization
establishes a central mail operation to receive acquisitions packages from vendors, exchange
partners, donors, and publishers.  Copyright receipts continue to be received from Copyright.  As
the result of these arrangements:

1. purchased materials are addressed directly to the purchasing unit and cataloged within that
unit;
2. CIP materials are addressed to the U.S. and Publisher Liaison Division (USPL); some material
is cataloged in that division and some is forwarded to the U.S. General Division (USGen) for
cataloging in that division;
3. Copyright materials are sent to USPL; some material is cataloged in that division and some is
forwarded to USGen for cataloging in that division;
4. material from overseas offices is sent to the relevant division; most will have received
cataloging in the originating office; cataloging and/or processing is completed in the relevant
division.
Thus there is a strong emphasis upon completing processing of material in the single unit that acquires
and receives it, thus reducing redundancies and exceptions to the extent possible.
This appendix describes workflows for
1. new full cataloging of monographs including items done through CIP;
2. items done under cross-team cataloging;
3. added volumes;
4. CIP verification/upgrade;
5. minimal level cataloging.
Some terminology is new; it is explained at the end of this appendix.
