STARS is a PC-based statistical software package developed specifically to gather statistical
information about cataloging activity in a systematic manner. The technical details of using the
system are given in documentation developed especially for this purpose. See DCM B5.7
(Appendix 2) for details of application in the team. The term used for STARS activity is
"logging" to distinguish this activity from "charging," which relates solely to the location of an
item.  Items processed through a team are logged in and out of STARS at the appropriate time.
In general, the data recorded are LCCN/RID, priority, language, format (e.g., book, serial,
microform), activity to be performed (e.g., new full cataloging, CIP verification/upgrade, MLC),
date of receipt, date of completion, and extent of completion (e.g., descriptive aspect only). See
also DCM B6 (Referrals/Forwarding).
Recording incoming items is called "logging in"; recording outgoing items, "logging out."
After the initial screening process, all items accepted by the team are logged in. When the item
leaves the team either fully or partially completed (e.g., items undergoing cross-team
cataloging), it is logged out, giving the date and extent of completion. *Who* does the logging
(whether catalogers themselves, team distributors, work/study students, messengers, or any other
suitable arrangement) *and at what point in the process* is at the discretion of the team, .
